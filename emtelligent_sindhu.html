Buyer personas → messaging framework

Emtelligent's buyers are not the same person.

Two distinct buyers. Different motivations, different fears, different language. The message that lands with a VP of Data will lose a Clinical Informatics Officer in the first sentence — and vice versa.

VD
VP of Data
National payer / health system
Core job to be done
Make unstructured clinical data usable for analytics, risk models, and AI — without building a custom NLP team or depending on unreliable black-box outputs.
What they care about
Accuracy at scale EHR integration HIPAA / compliance Throughput benchmarks ROI on data infra
Their fear
AI that hallucinates clinical codes and exposes the org to liability. They've been burned by overpromised ML vendors before. They need proof, not demos.
Message that lands

"Deterministic, auditable NLP — not a black box. 99% benchmark accuracy on 5B+ clinical notes. Your compliance team will sleep at night."

CI
Clinical Informatics Officer
Health system / IDN
Core job to be done
Bridge clinical workflows and data systems — surface the right information to the right stakeholder without forcing manual chart review or disrupting how clinicians already work.
What they care about
Clinician trust ICD / SNOMED coding Time to value Workflow fit 10+ therapeutic areas
Their fear
A tool clinicians ignore because it misreads context or adds friction. Trust is hard to rebuild once broken. They want to see real document types — discharge summaries, op reports, path notes — handled correctly.
Message that lands

"Clinically validated NLP trained across 10+ therapeutic areas and real document types. Fits how your teams already work — no workflow redesign required."

Market positioning → competitive landscape

Where Emtelligent wins — and why it matters.

The clinical NLP market is fragmented. Most players are either too broad, too voice-focused, or too dev-heavy for payer and analytics use cases. Emtelligent's white space is the deterministic infrastructure layer.

Emtelligent Nuance / Microsoft AWS Comprehend Medical John Snow Labs
Primary focus Clinical NLP → structured coded data for payers & analytics Ambient voice, CDI, clinical scribe tools General medical entity extraction via API Medical NLP library + pretrained models
Output type Deterministic, auditable Partial — voice-to-text driven Probabilistic scores Model-dependent
Payer / analytics fit Core market Not primary Possible, dev-heavy Build your own pipeline
Speed to value API-first, fast integration Requires full MS stack AWS infra dependency Engineering-heavy setup
Benchmark accuracy 99% — 23 pts above industry Not publicly benchmarked General purpose, variable Model and config dependent
Startup agility Highly customizable, seed-stage speed Enterprise product cycles Cloud platform constraints OSS, community-paced
Positioning opportunity

Nuance owns voice and ambient documentation. AWS owns generic extraction. John Snow Labs serves data science teams who want to build their own. Emtelligent's unclaimed territory is deterministic, payer-ready NLP — the infrastructure layer between raw clinical documents and downstream AI. That's a category creation play, not just a feature comparison. The 99% accuracy number and 2M+ expert-annotated data points are the proof points that make the category credible.
